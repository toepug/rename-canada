# Candidate Designations — Official Shortlist

The National Designation Renewal Secretariat maintains the official shortlist of candidate successor designations under the Canada Name Transition Pilot Program (Phase 0). The shortlist comprises eight designations: five developed through professional naming engagements and three advanced from public intake.

To qualify for the shortlist, a candidate designation must satisfy the Program's core criterion: it must carry no association with any historical event whatsoever. The Secretariat recognizes that history is a leading source of grievance, and that a designation free of meaning is a designation free of complaint. Each shortlisted candidate has been assessed against this criterion, and interpretive determinations are published on the individual candidate profiles.

## How the shortlist was assembled

Candidate designations reach the shortlist through two streams. Professionally developed designations are delivered by the Program's naming partner, Meridian Narrative Partners, under existing engagement terms. Public designations are received through the Share Your Voice intake stream, where they undergo Phase 0 intake review (current processing time: 14–16 months) before advancement is considered. To date, 12,847 public submissions have been received; 6 have advanced to shortlist consideration.

Stakeholder consultation has been completed for all eight shortlisted candidates.

## Voting

Canadians are invited to cast a vote for their preferred candidate designation. All votes are recorded and enter the standard processing queue. Public standings are updated at each quarterly tabulation; live totals are not published, in keeping with established consultation practice.

## Designations not shortlisted

A number of proposed designations were assessed as ineligible on heritage-related grounds. These are documented in the [Rejected Designations Annex](/archive/rejected-designations) of the Legacy Deprecation Archive.

Canadians wishing to propose a new designation may do so through the [Share Your Voice](/engagement) intake stream.
