## 1.0 Mandate

The National Designation Renewal Secretariat coordinates the Government's designation renewal agenda. The Secretariat's mandate is to ensure that the national identifier transition proceeds in a manner that is orderly, evidence-informed, dignity-compliant, and phased.

The Secretariat is committed to plain language. Our mandate, in plain language, is to coordinate the renewal of designations, nationally.

## 2.0 What we do

The Secretariat delivers on its mandate through four core functions:

1. **Coordination.** The Secretariat coordinates across departments, vendors, and phases. It does not direct, decide, or implement, which are functions of other bodies, several of which will be established in future phases.
2. **Evaluation.** The Secretariat evaluates candidate designations against the Program's core criterion: a compliant national identifier must carry no association with any historical event whatsoever.
3. **Documentation.** Under TB Reg. 2024-117, s. 12(b), the Secretariat maintains a complete, accurate, and permanent record of every heritage asset scheduled for sunset — its origin, its history, its significance, and the people connected to it — prior to any deprecation action. The Secretariat regards this obligation as absolute. No element of the national past will be lost without first being fully preserved.
4. **Engagement.** The Secretariat receives the views of Canadians and retains them.

## 4.0 Accountability

As a secretariat, the NDRS coordinates the Program on behalf of the responsible authorities. Questions regarding the Program's direction should be addressed to the responsible authorities, whose identification is a Phase 1 deliverable. Until that phase, accountability for the Program rests with the framework, which the Secretariat administers but did not design, under a mandate it implements but did not author, on a timeline it maintains but does not control.

The Secretariat publishes a "Date modified" on every page as part of its commitment to transparency.

## 5.0 Program designation

The initiative is known publicly as the **Canada Name Transition Pilot Program (Phase 0)**. Its formal designation is the *National Identifier Sunset and Succession Program*, established under Treasury Board Regulation 2024-117. Vendor-side documentation may refer to the engagement as *Project Northstar: A National Renaming Journey*; this designation is Meridian's and the Secretariat has elected not to discuss it.

## 6.0 Contact us

The Secretariat welcomes correspondence from Canadians.

**Write to us:** Use the [Share Your Voice](/engagement) form. Submissions are processed in the order received. Current processing time: 14–16 months.

**Media inquiries:** Media inquiries are treated as submissions.

**Visit us:** The Secretariat does not currently maintain premises accessible to the public. Canadians wishing to visit are encouraged to visit the [Legacy Deprecation Archive](/archive), which is open at all times and contains everything.
