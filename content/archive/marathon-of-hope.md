The asset is a 5,373-kilometre run across more than half of Canada, completed in 143 days on a prosthetic leg, and the annual event that has carried its name and purpose into more than sixty countries since. It began and ended within a single year. This record documents both.

## 2.0 Historical record

*Documented per TB Reg. 2024-117, s. 12(b).*

### 2.1 A diagnosis and a decision (1977)

**Terry Fox**, 18, was diagnosed with osteosarcoma in his right leg in 1977; the leg was amputated above the knee. While in hospital, he resolved to run across Canada to raise money for cancer research, having met other young cancer patients on the ward and learned of Dick Traum, the first amputee to complete a marathon.

### 2.2 The Marathon of Hope (12 April – 1 September 1980)

Fox dipped his artificial leg in the Atlantic Ocean at St. John's, Newfoundland, on 12 April 1980, and began running west. He covered the equivalent of a full marathon, roughly 42 kilometres, on most running days, through Atlantic Canada, Quebec, and Ontario. Over 143 days he covered 5,373 kilometres — an average of approximately 37.6 kilometres a day — before chest pain forced him to stop just outside Thunder Bay, Ontario, on 1 September 1980. Along the way, he turned down every commercial endorsement he was offered.

### 2.3 28 June 1981

Terry Fox died in New Westminster, British Columbia, nine months after the cancer that ended his run was found to have spread to his lungs. He was 22.

### 2.4 The annual run (1981–present)

The first annual Terry Fox Run was held that September, weeks after his death. It has been held every year since, now in more than sixty countries, and is the world's largest one-day fundraiser for cancer research. The Terry Fox Foundation and the Terry Fox Research Institute have together raised over $1 billion for cancer research in his name to date. The Foundation maintains a standing policy, in place since the Marathon of Hope itself, against any corporate logo or sponsorship acknowledgment appearing on Run materials in any country.

## 3.0 Deprecation assessment

### 3.1 Naming and provenance exposure

The designation is inseparable from a named individual, a fixed 143-day span, and a specific cross-country route. Fact Density was assessed by Meridian as severe.

### 3.2 Institutional constraints (self-defeating finding)

The Terry Fox Foundation, which organizes the annual event continuing this designation's purpose, maintains a standing policy — in place since the Marathon of Hope itself — against any corporate logo, sponsorship acknowledgment, or commercial branding appearing on event materials in any jurisdiction. The Program's own toolkit of candidate designations, sponsor placeholders, and consultant-developed identity platforms is accordingly unavailable for use in connection with this asset, by the explicit and enduring policy of its own governing charity. No comparable action is available to the Secretariat.

### 3.3 Disposition

Sunset is warranted on the merits and cannot presently be executed. The asset has been placed in the **deferred queue**, where it will be reviewed quarterly, indefinitely, alongside a policy this Secretariat has no standing to waive. The Secretariat is satisfied that this constitutes progress.
